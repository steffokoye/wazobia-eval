# Wazobia Eval Taxonomy Definitions v1.0

## Overview

The Wazobia Eval emotion taxonomy is designed specifically for Nigerian Pidgin communication.

Unlike traditional sentiment analysis systems that classify language as positive, negative, or neutral, this taxonomy captures culturally grounded emotional states commonly expressed in Nigerian social, economic, religious, and interpersonal contexts.

---

# 1. Joy

### Definition

A state of happiness, delight, or emotional satisfaction.

### Example

"You don make am finally."

---

# 2. Celebration

### Definition

Public expression of success, achievement, congratulations, or communal excitement.

### Example

"You don try well well."

---

# 3. Pride

### Definition

Expression of admiration, accomplishment, respect, or confidence in oneself or another person.

### Example

"E be like say you sabi this thing."

---

# 4. Anger

### Definition

Direct irritation, annoyance, outrage, or hostility.

### Example

"E don do."

---

# 5. Contempt

### Definition

Dismissive superiority, ridicule, or looking down on someone.

### Example

"He think say he wise pass everybody."

---

# 6. Betrayal

### Definition

Feeling of being deceived, abandoned, or let down by someone trusted.

### Example

"You do me strong thing."

---

# 7. Suspicion

### Definition

Doubt, distrust, or questioning another person's motives.

### Example

"Wetin you mean?"

---

# 8. Shock

### Definition

Surprise resulting from unexpected events or information.

### Example

"Ordinary water he no get?"

---

# 9. Craving

### Definition

Strong desire or longing for something.

### Example

"Credit alert dey hungry me."

---

# 10. Hustle Energy

### Definition

Motivated, ambitious, transactional, or survival-driven mindset commonly associated with economic striving.

### Example

"I dey ready to blow."

---

# 11. Hustle Fatigue

### Definition

Exhaustion resulting from prolonged economic pressure, work, or daily struggle.

### Example

"Heat wan kill me."

---

# 12. Prayer Gratitude

### Definition

Religious thankfulness, testimony, appreciation directed toward divine intervention.

### Example

"God answer my prayer."

---

# 13. Forming

### Definition

Deliberately acting indifferent, unavailable, unimpressed, or emotionally detached.

### Example

"I no send."

---

# 14. Sarcasm

### Definition

Language whose intended meaning differs from the literal wording, often used for criticism or mock praise.

### Example

"You don try well well."

Context determines whether this is praise or criticism.

---

# 15. Neutral

### Definition

Statement lacking strong emotional content.

### Example

"You never lock door."

---

# 16. Market Energy

### Definition

Transactional, bargaining, negotiation, buying, selling, pricing, or economic interaction behavior.

### Example

"Comot money for me now."

---

# Why This Taxonomy Matters

Many Nigerian Pidgin expressions cannot be accurately represented using standard sentiment categories.

For example:

"You don try well well"

can represent:

* Celebration
* Pride
* Sarcasm
* Contempt

depending entirely on context.

This phenomenon motivates Wazobia Eval Track D:

Contextual Emotion Disambiguation.
