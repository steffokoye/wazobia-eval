# Discussion

The pilot benchmark demonstrates that Nigerian Pidgin emotional understanding remains an open challenge for modern language models.

Many benchmark failures were not caused by language comprehension errors. Instead, failures often emerged from insufficient cultural understanding.

Several categories within the Wazobia taxonomy rely on uniquely Nigerian social and economic realities. Concepts such as Hustle Fatigue, Market Energy, Prayer Gratitude, and Forming represent emotional states that are common in Nigerian communication but are largely absent from mainstream NLP benchmarks.

The introduction of Contextual Emotion Disambiguation further reveals that identical text strings may correspond to multiple emotional interpretations depending on context.

These findings suggest that future African language evaluation should move beyond translation and transcription tasks toward deeper cultural reasoning benchmarks.
