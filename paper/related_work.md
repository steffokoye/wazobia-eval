# Related Work

## Emotion Understanding in NLP

Emotion recognition has become an important task in natural language processing (NLP), enabling systems to identify emotional states expressed in text. Existing benchmarks such as GoEmotions provide fine-grained emotion labels for English-language datasets.

However, most emotion understanding research focuses on high-resource languages and assumes emotional categories that may not generalize across cultural contexts.

---

## African Language NLP

Recent years have seen growing interest in NLP for African languages. Initiatives such as Masakhane have contributed datasets, machine translation systems, speech resources, and language technologies across numerous African languages.

Despite this progress, evaluation resources remain limited compared to English and other high-resource languages.

---

## Nigerian Pidgin Resources

Nigerian Pidgin is one of Africa's most widely spoken languages, yet benchmark resources remain scarce.

Existing datasets primarily focus on:

* Machine Translation
* Speech Recognition
* Language Identification
* Text Classification

Few resources evaluate emotional understanding, sarcasm detection, or culturally grounded reasoning.

---

## Cultural Reasoning Challenges

Many Nigerian expressions are context-dependent and culturally specific.

For example:

"You don try well well"

may express:

* celebration
* pride
* sarcasm
* contempt

depending on speaker intent and social context.

Traditional sentiment analysis frameworks struggle to capture these distinctions.

---

## Contribution of Wazobia Eval

Wazobia Eval introduces:

1. A culturally grounded Nigerian Pidgin emotion taxonomy.

2. Benchmark tasks for emotion classification.

3. Sarcasm detection evaluation.

4. Cultural reasoning evaluation.

5. Contextual Emotion Disambiguation (Track D).

To our knowledge, Wazobia Eval is among the first benchmarks specifically designed to evaluate emotional and cultural understanding in Nigerian Pidgin.
