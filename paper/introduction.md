# Introduction

Recent advances in large language models have transformed natural language processing. Modern systems demonstrate impressive capabilities in reasoning, translation, summarization, and conversational understanding. However, evaluation benchmarks remain heavily concentrated on English and a small number of high-resource languages.

African languages remain significantly underrepresented in evaluation infrastructure. While datasets for translation, speech recognition, and language identification have expanded in recent years, benchmark resources for emotional understanding and cultural reasoning remain scarce.

Nigerian Pidgin presents a particularly challenging evaluation setting. Spoken by tens of millions of people across Nigeria and beyond, the language relies heavily on cultural context, shared social understanding, tone, and pragmatic cues. Many expressions cannot be accurately interpreted through literal translation alone.

For example, the expression "You don try well well" may communicate praise, pride, sarcasm, contempt, or celebration depending on context. Traditional sentiment analysis frameworks struggle to capture these distinctions.

To address this gap, we introduce Wazobia Eval, a benchmark designed specifically for Nigerian Pidgin emotional understanding and cultural reasoning. The benchmark introduces a culturally grounded emotion taxonomy, multiple evaluation tracks, and a novel contextual disambiguation task.

Our goal is to provide researchers and practitioners with a standardized framework for evaluating Nigerian Pidgin language understanding beyond transcription and translation.
