# Abstract

Large language models have demonstrated strong performance across many natural language understanding tasks. However, evaluation resources remain limited for African languages, particularly Nigerian Pidgin, one of the most widely spoken languages in Africa.

We introduce Wazobia Eval, a benchmark designed to evaluate emotional understanding, sarcasm detection, cultural reasoning, and contextual emotion disambiguation in Nigerian Pidgin. The benchmark is grounded in a culturally informed emotion taxonomy consisting of sixteen categories derived from Nigerian linguistic and social contexts.

Wazobia Eval includes benchmark tasks for emotion classification, sarcasm detection, cultural reasoning, and a novel Contextual Emotion Disambiguation track that evaluates whether models can correctly interpret identical expressions appearing in different contexts.

Initial pilot evaluations reveal substantial performance gaps between surface-level language understanding and culturally grounded interpretation. These findings suggest that existing frontier language models continue to struggle with context-dependent emotional reasoning in Nigerian Pidgin.

Wazobia Eval aims to provide researchers, developers, and organizations with a standardized evaluation framework for measuring Nigerian Pidgin language understanding and advancing African language AI systems.
